About the Estate

Founded in
Curiosity

Mycelial Estate began in a 10×12 foot grow tent in southern Indiana. What started as an experiment in substrate and strain selection grew into a climate-controlled laboratory and farm producing gourmet mushrooms year-round.

Mycelial Estate · Indiana farm and lab

Our Story

From Tent to
Laboratory

Mycelial Estate was founded with a simple question: could we grow restaurant-quality mushrooms without hardwood sawdust, without synthetic inputs, and without the waste typically associated with commercial substrate production?

After two years of trials across 30+ substrate formulations, we landed on a pasteurized straw and reclaimed cardboard system that produces consistently dense, flavorful oyster mushrooms. Today we operate a dedicated lab with laminar flow hoods, autoclave sterilization, and climate-controlled fruiting chambers.

We're based in Indiana, where we source our straw from local farms and our cardboard from area businesses. Every block of spent substrate is composted on-site and returned to the soil.

What We Believe

Our Values

Regenerative by Design

We don't just grow mushrooms — we build soil. Our substrate system is designed so that nothing goes to landfill. Straw becomes mushrooms, mushrooms become compost, and compost feeds the next generation of plants.

Precision Over Scale

Rather than maximizing output per square foot, we optimize for flavor, texture, and consistency. Each strain is grown under conditions tuned to its biology. We harvest at peak flush, not peak volume.

Transparent Craft

We document every batch — substrate formula, colonization time, flush weight, and sensory notes. That data informs our next grow and builds a body of knowledge we share openly.
