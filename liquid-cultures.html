Quality & Protocol

Lab-Prepared, Double-Verified

Master Culture Library

Every liquid culture originates from a master culture plate maintained in our culture library. Master cultures are stored on slants at 40 °F and refreshed every 90 days to maintain genetic vigor. No serial transfers — no genetic drift.

Sterile Media Preparation

Our liquid culture media uses organic malt extract and peptone dissolved in distilled water, sterilized at 15 PSI for 30 minutes. Media is cooled, pH-adjusted to 5.5, and inoculated under laminar flow. Each batch is visually clear before use.

Microscope Verification

Before any syringe leaves the lab, a sample is examined under 400x magnification. We check for typical hyphal morphology, clamp connections, and the complete absence of bacterial rods, yeast budding, or sporulating molds.

Agar Plate Confirmation

A parallel sample is streaked onto malt extract agar and incubated for 7 days. Only syringes that produce clean, characteristic growth — no bacterial or fungal contaminants — are released for sale.

Questions

Liquid Culture FAQs

How many grains can one syringe inoculate? +
A 10 mL syringe will inoculate 3–5 quart jars of sterilized rye grain. Inject 1–2 mL per jar through the filter patch or an injection port. Shake immediately to distribute the culture.
Can I expand the culture to make more LC? +
Yes — inject 1 mL into a sterile jar containing 200–300 mL of prepared LC media. Use a stir plate at low speed for 5–7 days. Maintain sterile technique throughout to avoid contamination.
How should I store the syringe? +
Refrigerate at 35–45 °F. Use within 6 months for best viability. Allow the syringe to warm to room temperature for 2 hours before use. Do not freeze.
Can I use this on agar plates? +
Absolutely — place 1–2 drops onto the center of a malt extract agar plate and spread with a sterile loop. Visible growth typically appears within 3–5 days at 75 °F.
