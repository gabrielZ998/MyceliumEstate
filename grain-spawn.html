Quality & Process

Lab-Grade Spawn for Home & Farm

Our Grain

We use certified organic rye grain — small, round kernels with a tough bran layer that resists bursting during hydration. The grain is simmered to the proper moisture content, drained, and loaded into wide-mouth quart jars before autoclave sterilization.

Sterile Protocol

Every step from grain prep to inoculation happens under HEPA-filtered laminar flow. Jars are sterilized at 15 PSI for 90 minutes, cooled for 24 hours, then inoculated with a master culture plate. We reject any jar that shows signs of contamination during incubation.

Colonization & Testing

After inoculation, jars are kept at 75 °F in a dark incubation room. Full colonization takes 14–21 days depending on the strain. Before shipping, each jar is inspected visually and sampled to agar to confirm purity.

Using Your Spawn

Break the colonized grain into individual kernels and mix into your prepared substrate at a 5–10% spawn rate. Use within 4–6 weeks of receipt if refrigerated. Do not freeze. Allow jars to rest at room temperature for 24 hours before spawning.

Questions

Grain Spawn FAQs

How much substrate will one quart colonize? +
A fully colonized quart jar will inoculate 5–10 lbs of prepared substrate (straw, sawdust, cardboard). Use a 5–10% spawn rate for best results — more spawn means faster colonization and lower contamination risk.
How should I store my spawn? +
Keep refrigerated at 35–45 °F. Do not freeze. Use within 4–6 weeks of receipt. Let the jar sit at room temperature for 24 hours before spawning to wake the mycelium.
Can I use this spawn for agar or liquid culture? +
Yes — you can transfer a small piece of colonized grain to an agar plate to isolate clean mycelium, or drop a grain into sterile liquid culture media to create a master culture.
Do you ship internationally? +
Currently we ship to the contiguous United States only. International shipping for live cultures is restricted by customs regulations in many countries.
