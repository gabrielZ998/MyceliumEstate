Our Methods

A Regenerative
Practice

Every variety on the estate is grown on pasteurized straw and reclaimed cardboard — abundant, low-impact materials that would otherwise be discarded. There's no hardwood sawdust, no virgin timber, and no synthetic inputs in our substrate. When a block finishes fruiting, the spent substrate doesn't go to a landfill: it's broken down and returned to the soil as mushroom compost, closing the loop on nearly everything we grow with.

Primary InputsStraw & Reclaimed Cardboard
Spent SubstrateComposted On-Site
Waste DivertedNear-Zero
Compost UseGarden & Farm Soil Amendment

The Blue Oyster
Up Close

Pleurotus columbinus

Our highest-yielding strain and the one we recommend for first-time buyers. The blue oyster is forgiving in the kitchen, fast in the fruiting chamber, and consistent across every flush. We grow it year-round at 60–68 °F with high airflow to keep the caps tight and the color vivid.

SubstratePasteurized Wheat Straw
Temp Range60–68 °F fruiting
Flush Cycle6–9 days, 2–3 flushes
FormatFresh · Dried · Grain Spawn · Cultures
