Our Approach

How We
Grow

Every cluster that leaves our farm begins as a spore on an agar plate in our laboratory. From there it moves through grain, substrate, and fruiting chamber — each stage controlled, measured, and documented.

Laboratory · sterile culture work

Phase 1

Culture & Spawn

We maintain a master culture library of every strain we grow. Cultures are stored on agar slants at 40 °F and refreshed every 90 days to prevent genetic drift. When a production run begins, we transfer from the master to fresh plates, then to sterile rye grain jars.

All grain preparation and inoculation is performed under HEPA-filtered laminar flow. Jars are sterilized at 15 PSI for 90 minutes, cooled, and inoculated with clean mycelium. Full colonization takes 14–21 days depending on the strain.

Substrate · straw & cardboard preparation

Phase 2

Substrate Preparation

Our primary substrate is wheat straw sourced from farms within 50 miles of the estate. We chop the straw to 2–4 inch lengths and blend in reclaimed cardboard from local businesses — clean cardboard that would otherwise go to landfill.

The mixture is loaded into mesh bags and submerged in a hot water bath at 160 °F for 90 minutes. This pasteurization kills competitor molds while preserving beneficial thermophilic bacteria that help protect the spawn. After cooling, the substrate is hydrated to 65–70% moisture and packed into filter bags.

Fruiting chambers · climate controlled

Phase 3

Colonization & Fruiting

Inoculated substrate bags are moved to a dark incubation room held at 75 °F. Mycelium colonizes the straw over 10–18 days. Once fully white, bags are transferred to the fruiting chamber where conditions shift to trigger pin formation.

Our fruiting chamber maintains 60–68 °F with 90–95% humidity and high airflow. CO\u2082 is kept below 900 ppm. Lighting runs 12 hours on a timer to guide cap development. Clusters are harvested at peak flush — when caps are fully formed but before spore drop.

Compost · closing the loop

Phase 4

Compost & Regeneration

After 2–3 flushes, spent substrate blocks are broken open and added to our compost piles. The fully colonized straw breaks down rapidly, reaching internal temperatures of 140+ °F during the active composting phase.

The finished compost is used as soil amendment in the estate's garden beds and distributed to local community gardens. Nothing goes to landfill — the straw that fed the mushrooms feeds the soil that grows next year's cover crops.

Full Process

From Spore to Spent Block

01

Master Culture

Strain selected from library and transferred to fresh agar. Incubated 7–10 days at 75 °F.

02

Grain Spawn

Sterilized rye grain inoculated from agar. Colonized 14–21 days. Tested for purity before use.

03

Substrate Inoculation

Grain spawn mixed into pasteurized straw at 5–10% spawn rate. Packed into filter bags.

04

Incubation

Bags held at 75 °F in darkness for 10–18 days until fully colonized.

05

Fruiting

Bags moved to fruiting chamber — 60–68 °F, 90–95% humidity, 12 hr light cycle. Pins form in 5–7 days.

06

Harvest

Clusters hand-cut at peak flush. Sorted, lightly cleaned, packed same-day for shipment or pickup.

07

Compost

Spent substrate broken down in compost piles. Returned to soil as finished compost.
